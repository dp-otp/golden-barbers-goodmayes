Low Fade

Fade begins just above the ear and around the neckline. Subtle, professional — holds its shape longest.

⏱ Every 4–5 weeks

Starts at the temple. The most popular choice — balanced between sharp and conservative. Suits all face shapes.

Fade goes high — near the temples and above. Maximum contrast, bold and modern. Needs the most upkeep.

⏱ Every 2–3 weeks

  • Low skin fade — clean, subtle, great for work environments
  • Mid fade bald — the most common request, looks sharp at all times
  • High taper fade — taper doesn't go to skin, softer finish
  • Burst fade — curves around the ear, often used with mohawks or twists
  • Drop fade — dips behind the ear, gives a curved silhouette from the back

Beard Grooming

A great haircut and an untidy beard cancel each other out. Here's how to keep both locked in.

In the Chair

What Happens During a Beard Trim

A proper beard trim at Golden Barbers isn't just running a trimmer over your face. We shape the cheek line, define the neckline, blend the length, and use a straight razor for crisp edges.

Cheek line shaped to complement your face
Neckline defined — above Adam's apple, clean natural curve
Length blended so it tapers into the fade
Straight razor edges for that sharp finish
Beard oil or balm applied at the end

🧴
Washing Your Beard
Wash 2–3 times a week with a dedicated beard wash — regular shampoo strips the natural oils and leaves beard hair brittle and itchy. Condition after every wash.
Routine: 2–3x per week
💧
Beard Oil
Apply 3–5 drops after washing while slightly damp. Massaged into skin first, then through the hair. Moisturises both the skin underneath and the beard itself — prevents itch and flaking.
Routine: Daily
🪮
Brushing & Combing
Boar bristle brush trains hairs to grow downward and distributes oil through the beard. Comb for styling and detangling. Both used together give you that full, even look.
Routine: Daily
✂️
Home Maintenance
Trim stray hairs with scissors when they escape the line. Keep your neckline clean between visits with a trimmer — go no lower than two fingers above the Adam's apple.
Routine: Weekly

⚠️ Common beard mistakes

  • Setting the neckline too high — creates a double chin effect
  • Not conditioning — leads to split ends and wire-like texture
  • Trimming too short when wet — beard looks longer when wet, always trim dry
  • Skipping the cheek line — even a natural look needs a clean top border

Ear Flaming

One of the most misunderstood services we offer — and one of the most satisfying once you've had it.

The Technique

What Is Ear Flaming?

Ear flaming (also called ear candling or pyrotrichosis treatment) uses a controlled, quick flame to burn away the fine, unwanted hair that grows on and around the outer ear.

It sounds intense — but it's completely painless. The flame is passed across the skin so quickly that you only feel a brief warmth. No razors, no tweezers, no pain.

Takes 30–60 seconds per ear
Completely painless — brief warmth only
Results last longer than shaving or trimming

Section 06

Product Guide

What you use at home determines how well your cut holds between visits. Here's what actually works.

💊
Pomade
Water-based: flexible hold, easy wash out. Oil-based: stronger hold, higher shine. Use for slick backs, pompadours, side parts.
🧱
Clay
Matte finish, strong hold. Best for textured crops, quiffs and messy styles. Apply to dry hair for maximum texture.
🫧
Wax
Pliable hold, natural finish. Great for definition without stiffness. Good for short to medium styles and daily wear.
💨
Sea Salt Spray
Creates beach texture and volume on damp hair. Ideal for wavy or curly hair. Spray in, scrunch, air dry or diffuse.
💧
Beard Oil
Moisturises skin and hair. Prevents itch and flaking. Use 3–5 drops daily — jojoba and argan oil are the best base ingredients.
🔒
Beard Balm
Like beard oil but with hold. Tames flyaways and shapes longer beards. Use it to train your beard direction over time.
🧼
Beard Wash
Never use regular shampoo on your beard — it strips natural oils. Dedicated beard wash keeps the skin underneath healthy.
🌿
Scalp Serum
If you're experiencing thinning or hair loss — scalp serums with biotin, caffeine or minoxidil applied directly are the most effective early intervention.

💡 Application tips

  • Less is more — start with a pea-sized amount and build up
  • Apply clay and wax to dry hair; pomade and sea salt to damp hair
  • Warm product between your palms before working through the hair
  • Ask us in the chair — we'll tell you exactly what we used and recommend what to buy
Section 07

Barber Vocabulary

Walk in knowing the language. Use these exact terms and your barber knows exactly what you mean.

Skin Fade / Bald Fade
Fade that goes all the way down to the skin. No stubble at the bottom.
Taper
Gradual length reduction — doesn't go to skin. Softer, more conservative finish.
Lineup / Edge Up
Straight razor or trimmer used to sharpen the hairline at forehead and temples.
Drop Fade
The fade line curves down and drops behind the ear — creates a curved silhouette.
Burst Fade
Fade fans out around the ear in a semi-circle. Often used with mohawks or twists.
Temple Fade
Fade focused specifically on the temple area. Used to clean up the sides above the ear.
Guard Number
The clipper guard setting — 1 is shortest (3mm), 4 is medium (13mm), 8 is longest (25mm).
Scissor Over Comb
Technique to cut longer hair on top — comb holds, scissors cut. Gives a softer, blended result than clippers.
Blending
Connecting two different lengths so the transition is invisible — the core skill of every good fade.
Neckline
The shape at the bottom of the haircut. Can be squared, rounded, tapered or faded.
Wax / Neck Wax
Hot wax applied to ears, nose or neck to remove unwanted hair cleanly.
Part
A defined line cut or combed into the hair. Can be done with a comb, razor or clipper.
